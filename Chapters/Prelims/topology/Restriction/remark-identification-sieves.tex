\begin{remark}\label[remark]{cc0}
Let $\cat{C}$ be a category 
and $a,b \in \cat{C}$.
Let $f:b\rightarrow a \in \overcat{C}{a}$.
The map $\maxsieve{f} \rightarrow \maxsieve{b}$ sending 
a morphism to $f$ to its underlying morphism in $\cat{C}$
is a bijection. Moreover composition in $\cat{C}$ and $\overcat{C}{a}$ are the same,
so this identification respects pullback of sieves.
This observation yields us that $\sievecat{b} = \sievecat{f}$.

Whenever $R$ is a sieve on $b$, we will denote the corresponding sieve on $f$ by $R_f$.
\end{remark}
