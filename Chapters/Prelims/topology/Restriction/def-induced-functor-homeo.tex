\begin{definition}\label[definition]{cd3}
Let $\rsite{C}{T}{O}$ be a ringed site.
Let $a,b\in \cat{C}$ and $f:a\rightarrow b$.

Consider the induced functor $\overcat{C}{a} \xrightarrow{F} \overcat{C}{b}$.
Let $c$ be the pullback of some pair $d\rightarrow g \leftarrow e$.
Then $F(c)$ is the pullback of the pair $F(d) \rightarrow F(g) \leftarrow F(e)$,
so $F$ preserves pullbacks.

Let $c\rightarrow b\in \overcat{C}{b}$.
Let $R$ be a covering sieve on $c$.
By \Cref{bb2} and the fact that $u\circ F = u$, we get that $F(R)$ generates a covering sieve, so $F$ is continuous. With the same reasoning one can deduce that $F$ lifts covers.

Let $\sheaf{O}$ be the structure sheaf of $\overcat{C}{a}$
and $\sheaf{U}$ be the structure sheaf of $\overcat{C}{b}$.
Note that $\sect{c\xrightarrow{g}b\xrightarrow{f}a}{O} = \sect{c}{O} = \sect{g}{U}$.
Define the morphism of ringed sites 
$(F,\id): \rsite{\overcat{C}{a}}{T_a}{O} \rightarrow \rsite{\overcat{C}{b}}{T_b}{O}$, which we will denote by $F$ or just $f$.
\end{definition}
