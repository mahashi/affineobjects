\begin{definition}[Pullback of sieve]\label[definition]{961}
Let $\cat{C}$ be a category
and $a,b \in \cat{C}$.
Let $S$ be a sieve on $a$.
Let $f:b \rightarrow a$.
The sieve $\spull{f}S$
on $b$ is given by
$\spull{f}S = \set{g \in \max{b} \with fg \in S}$
for any $c \in \cat{C}$.
To show that this is actually a sieve on $b$,
let $k: c \rightarrow c'$ 
and $h \in \spull{f}S$. 
Hence $fh \in S$ and so $fhk \in S$.
Conclude that $hk \in \spull{f}S$.
This defines a functor $\spull{f}: \sievecat{a} \rightarrow \sievecat{b}$.
\end{definition}
