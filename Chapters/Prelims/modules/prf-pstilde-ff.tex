\begin{proof}
Let $M,N$ be an $R$-module.
Let $a$ be an object of $C$. 
Let $f:a \rightarrow 1$.
Let $\varphi: \pstilde{M} \rightarrow \pstilde{N}$ be a morphism of presheaf modules.

Let $\pstilde{\varphi_1} = g : \pstilde{M} \rightarrow \pstilde{N}$.
Let $m \tensor r \in M \tensor_R \sheaf{O}(a)$. 
Then $g(m \tensor r) = \varphi_1(m) \tensor r$
and 

\begin*{align}
	\varphi_a(m \tensor r) &= r \varphi_a(m \tensor 1) \reason{linearity} \\
		&= r \varphi_a(\pstilde(M)(f)(m)) \\
		&= r \pstilde(N)(f)(\varphi_1(m)) \reason{naturality of transformation}\\
		&= r (\varphi_1(m) \tensor 1) \\
		&= \varphi_1(m) \tensor r.
\end*{align}

Hence $g = \varphi$.

Let $g,h: M\rightarrow N$. Assume that $g\neq h$. Then $\pstilde(g)_1 = g \neq h = \pstilde(h)$.
Hence $\pstilde$ is full and faithful.

\end{proof}
