\begin{definition}[Presheaf modules]\label[definition]{720}
Let $\rsite{C}{T}{O}$ be a ringed site.

A presheaf module on this ringed site is a presheaf of sets $\presheaf{F}$ 
on $\cat{C}$ together with a map of presheaves

\[ \presheaf{O} \times \presheaf{F} \rightarrow \presheaf{F}\]

such that for every object $a \in C$ 
the map $\sections{a}{O} \times \sections{a}{F} \rightarrow \sections{a}{F}$
defines a $\sections{a}{O}$-module structure on $\sections{a}{F}$.

A morphism $$\presheaf{F} \rightarrow \presheaf{G}$$ is a morphism of presheaf modules if 

\begin{center}
 	\begin{tikzcd}[row sep = large, column sep = large]
		\presheaf{O} \times \presheaf{F} \arrow{r}\arrow{d}
		& \presheaf{F} \arrow{d} \\
		\presheaf{O} \times \presheaf{G}\arrow{r}
		& \presheaf{G} \\
 	\end{tikzcd}
 \end{center}

commutes.
The category of presheaf modules on $\cat{C}$ will be denoted $\pshmodules{\sheaf{O}}$.
\end{definition}
