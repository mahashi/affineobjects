\begin{definition}[Tilde functor]\label[definition]{91d}
Let $\Spec{R}$ be an affine scheme.
Let $M$ be a $R$-module.
Define $\widetilde{M}$ to be the unique sheaf on $\Spec{R}$ with
\[
\wt{M}:D(f) \mapsto M_f.
\]
See \cite[Tag 01HR]{stacks}.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}\label[remark]{927}
This functor(with this notation) is commonly used in algebraic geometry texts.
By \cite[Tag 01I7]{stacks} and uniqueness of left adjoints $\stilde$ and $\widetilde{\blank}$ are canonically isomorphic on spaces, hence on small Zariski sites.
We will only use $\stilde$ in subsequent sections.
\end{remark}
