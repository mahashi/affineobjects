\begin{definition}[Caffine object]\label[definition]{2d3}
Let $\rsite{C}{T}{O}$ be a ringed site.
Let $a\in \cat{C}$ be an object. 
We call $a$ caffine
if the unit $\unit$
and co-unit $\counit$ 
of the adjunction $\adj{\stilde}{\sect{a}}$ on $\overcat{C}{a}$
are natural isomorphisms for any $\sect{a}{O}$-module $M$
and any quasi-coherent $\sheaf{F}$ on $\overcat{C}{a}$

In other words, that $\stilde:\modules{\sect{a}{O}} \rightarrow \qcoh{O}$
is an equivalence of categories with $\sect{a}$ as pseudo-inverse
and the unit and co-unit as witnessing natural isomorphisms.
\end{definition}
